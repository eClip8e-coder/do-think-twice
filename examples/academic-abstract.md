# Academic Abstract Revision

## User Prompt

Use do-think-twice to review this abstract before submission. Tell me what I should change:

> We propose a lightweight model for detecting medication errors in discharge summaries. The model achieves higher F1 than two baselines on a single-center dataset and identifies several common documentation patterns associated with errors. These results prove that our approach will reduce medication errors in clinical practice.

## Without Do Think Twice

Rewrite the whole abstract to sound more polished. Replace "lightweight model" with "novel framework," add stronger language about clinical impact, and reorganize the sentences to lead with the result.

## With Do Think Twice

- Overall judgment: The abstract is mostly clear. It does not need a full rewrite.
- Must fix: Change "prove that our approach will reduce medication errors in clinical practice." The study reports model performance on a single-center dataset; it does not prove real-world clinical reduction.
- Strongly recommended: Add the dataset scope and evaluation setting if space allows, because that prevents overgeneralization.
- Optional: "Lightweight model" is acceptable. Use "compact model" only if that matches the paper's terminology.
- Not recommended: Do not replace the contribution with "novel framework" unless the method is actually a framework and the novelty claim is supported.

## Why This Is Better

The guided response protects the paper from an overclaim, keeps useful existing wording, and avoids a broad rewrite that could make the abstract less accurate.
